INDIAN FESTIVELS

DEEPAVALI

Diwali was celebrated as a signifier of triumph of good over evil after Krishna's Victory over Narakasura. The day before Diwali is remembered as Naraka Chaturdashi, the day on which Narakasura was killed by Krishna.

Onam

Onam is an annual harvest and cultural festival celebrated mainly in the Indian state of Kerala. It commemorates the homecoming of the mythical King Mahabali and is known for its vibrant floral decorations (Pookalam), elaborate feasts (Onasadya), boat races (Vallam Kali), and other cultural performances. The ten-day festival brings Keralites together for feasting, music, dance, and traditional games, symbolizing unity, prosperity, and the spirit of the harvest season.

Holi

Holi is a vibrant Hindu festival, celebrated mainly in India, marking the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil. It's known as the "Festival of Colours" because participants throw bright powders and colored water at each other, engage in singing and dancing, and participate in community feasting and celebrations.
